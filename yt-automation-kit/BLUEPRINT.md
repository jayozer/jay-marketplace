## Step 1 — Research: find topics that are *already* winning
Don't guess what to make. Pull what's working in your niche and model it.

**Do this:** list 5–10 channels in your niche. Pull their recent videos sorted by views. Look for the **outlier** — the video that out-performed the channel's average. That topic has proven demand.

**AI prompt:**
> "Here are 20 recent videos from channels in [niche] with their view counts and titles. Identify the 3 topics that over-performed relative to each channel's norm, and tell me *why* each one hooked people."

*(My `yt-search` skill does this automatically — pulls + ranks the data for me.)*

## Step 2 — Title + SEO: engineer the click
A great video with a weak title dies. Reverse-engineer titles that already work.

**Do this:** take the top 5 videos on your topic. Find the pattern (number? curiosity gap? big promise?). Write 10 titles in that pattern, then cut to the 2 strongest.

**AI prompt:**
> "Here are the 5 top-performing titles on [topic]. Extract the pattern, then write me 10 titles for *my* video following it — each with a specific number or curiosity hook. Score each 1–10 for click-through."

*(My `seo` skill scores titles + builds the description and tags from competitive research.)*

## Step 3 — Plan + script: never face a blank page
Turn the topic into a hook, structure, and full script before you ever hit record.

**Do this:** feed the AI a reference video's transcript + your unique angle. Get a hook (first 15 seconds), a beat-by-beat outline, and a full script in your voice.

**AI prompt:**
> "Here's a transcript of a top video on [topic], and here's my angle: [your take]. Write me: a 15-second hook, a 6-beat outline, and a full script in a [casual/direct] voice. Flag the retention risk in each section."

*(My `yt` skill turns a reference + my angle into titles, hooks, the full script, and a filming guide.)*

## Step 4 — Thumbnail: stop the scroll
The thumbnail sells the click as hard as the title.

**Do this:** describe the emotion + the single clearest visual idea. Generate 3–4 variants, pick the one readable at a glance on mobile.

**AI prompt (for an image model):**
> "YouTube thumbnail, [emotion] expression, bold 3-word text '[X]', high contrast, one clear focal subject, readable at small size."

*(My `thumbnail` skill generates variants with Kie.ai — Nano Banana / Seedream.)*

## Step 5 — Repurpose: one video → a week of content
The video is the *least* of the content. Slice it.

**Do this:** pull 3 standalone moments → vertical Shorts/Reels/TikToks. Turn the core idea → an X thread, a LinkedIn post, a community post.

**AI prompt:**
> "From this video transcript, pull the 3 most clip-worthy 30–45s moments (with hooks), then write me 1 X thread, 1 LinkedIn post, and 1 short community post from the main idea."

*(My `shorts`, `repurpose`, and `content` skills do all of this from one transcript.)*
